A word on German pronunciation, German words are pronunced as they are spelt, i.e. each letter and each combination of letters stand for one sound only (not as in English where the same letter may have different sounds, e.g. the a in hat, far, and late). Once the few simple rules given below are mastered, it will be possible to pronounce every German word correctly.

German is spoken with more emphasis than English, and every syllable is more distincly sounded. Take for instance the word marmalade which in German is Marmelade. It is a word of four syllables, which in spoken English is reduced to two, marm-lid or /ˈmärməˌlād/. In German all four syllables are distinctly pronounced: mar-me-la-de.

There are no silent letters with the exception of h when followed by a constant. Thus the k in Knie is distinctly sounded and so is the p in Psychologie.

Usually the first syllable is stressed more heavily than the others. In words beginning with be-, ge-, er-, ver-, zer- the stress is on the second syllable. Many foreign words retain the stress of their original pronounciation and are pronounced as in their original language, e.g. Café and Restaurant are pronounced as in French, Jockey, Beefsteak as in English.
